About Me

My name is Xiaoning/Pepper Wang (汪小甯).I'm a PhD student at McGill University, as well as a passionate explorer at the intersection of technology and human experience.

Currently, I am engaged in research at McGill's ACT Research Group, supervised by Prof. Karyn Moffatt, as well as Prof. Amira Ghenai. My research interests focus on Human-AI interaction (HAI) and accessibility design for older adults. I am also interested in exploring people's cognitive dynamics and developing AI solutions to support and enhance people's interactions and experiences in digital environments.

Outside the academic sphere, my passions lie in literature and music. I am an avid reader, a novelist at heart, and a violinist by choice. These are not mere pastimes but integral parts of who I am, infusing my professional path with rich creativity and profound empathy.
