About Me

My name is Xiaoning/Pepper Wang (汪小甯). I am a PhD student at McGill University.

I am currently a member of McGill's ACT Research Group, supervised by Prof. Karyn Moffatt and Prof. Amira Ghenai. My research interests are in Human-Computer Interaction, accessibility, and Human-AI Interaction. I am especially interested in how AI systems can support older adults’ social and emotional experiences, including topics such as AI companionship and AI sycophancy.

Outside of research, I enjoy reading, writing, traveling, playing the violin, and going to the theater. I especially enjoy ballet, musicals, opera, and other live performances.
